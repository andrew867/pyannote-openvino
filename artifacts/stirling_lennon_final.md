## Interview Transcript

GEOFF: —your zodiac sign tell you how to manipulate the opposite sex to your advantage. I mean, there's no end to it. It seems to— when one's integrity goes in our system, there's no law.

JOHN: I think it's— there's never been any, as far as I can see. Has there? I mean, it's all started off on the wrong premise and has never been directed towards truth and integrity ever.

GEOFF: But it's getting to it, isn't it, in a way?

JOHN: Well, I mean, they all went to America to get away from the fascist King of England, you know, and look what happened. The Lord of the Flies. I don't know that one. The story of the boys being—

YOKO: Oh, on the island, yes, I know that, yes. Creating their own fascist organization because of the need to be told what to do to avoid making your own decisions.

JOHN: Decision-making is the thing that everybody tries to avoid. I mean, we do as well.

GEOFF: It's just so hard, you know.

JOHN: But if everybody was sort of facing the right way, it would be easier for all of us, whatever decisions we had to make, you know. If we had less fear, we could manage the world a bit better. I'm sure the next generation are going to do it, you know, because I know our generation's a damn sight better than the last one, morally, and, or whatever, you know. I'm getting stuck with these words. And the next one will be even better. So there's hope there, as long as we hold it off so that they can survive.

YOKO: That seems to be the aim right now, to keep leaning forward and hold it off.

JOHN: It's like Dick Gregory says, like the monster or the baddie or whatever it is, like he's so cocky now because he's been in control so long, he's getting careless, you know. Sort of performing his nasty deeds in front of people, you know, getting a bit loose, you know, like the sort of the bad cop, you know, gets a bit careless when he's making something on the side. And it seems like that. So there's an advantage to the machine being insane, as Dick Gregory calls it. Yes, yes. But it also might have disadvantage.

YOKO: Yes, it's an advantage if it doesn't become insane and destroy the total species. Yes, yes.

GEOFF: Before we get, you know, we reach the whatever core of it and do something.

YOKO: It's coming apart so fast at the seams. With our ability to communicate what's happening, I think we have a great deal of hope.

JOHN: Yes, I do.

YOKO: The beauty of the music that's laying on the message.

GEOFF: Well, despite all that, you know, both of you just kept saying about economics and all that. I still think that money is a concept, you know, and we can't rely on it. I mean, the only thing that we can rely on is manpower, you know. Mind-wise, you know, and every mind counts, and that's where the revolution starts really, not the money.

JOHN: We weren't saying it started with money.

GEOFF: No, no, but I mean, you know, money seems to be like playing an important role or something.

YOKO: Corrupting. Absolutely corrupting. The ability of money seems to be the thing, corrupting the ability of ego when it's out of control and misdirected.

GEOFF: But greed maybe. Because, you see, the average people get so discouraged, and they are discouraged, you know, because they feel that the situation is out of their hands. Even we feel that sometimes, so of course naturally, because I mean, we are in a better position sometimes, you know, when you think of it, because the publicity you get or whatever, you know. But it's not out of our hands, you know, it's like everybody counts. So that when you say, "Well, money is the force," then, you know, most of it, I mean, 90% of the people living in the country has nothing to do with it, then, you know, they can't do anything about it. Everybody can do it, you know.

YOKO: Gurdjieff said that 200 conscious beings could turn the world around.

GEOFF: Who said that?

YOKO: Gurdjieff.

GEOFF: Oh, Gurdjieff, yes. That's great, isn't it?

JOHN: Do you think there would be 200 in the world? Yeah. Pretty sad state if there isn't. I think there is now. Yeah, it's just getting it together, I suppose. It's so subtle again that it's—

GEOFF: No, but you see, playing by ear is very, very powerful, you know. Yes, in a way. Because even we don't know our plans, so how could they destroy our plan, stop it, you know? The next moment it's different.

JOHN: You don't really know what's going on, do we?
